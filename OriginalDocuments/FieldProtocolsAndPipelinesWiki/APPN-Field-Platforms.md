<p>
APPN field phenotyping platforms provide a versatile suite of UAV‑based sensor technologies and associated positioning and environmental instrumentation to support crop phenotyping projects at virtually any location. From paddock‑scale trials to detailed plot‑ and plant‑level analysis, APPN platforms support <strong>data capture, management, and analysis</strong> across diverse research and industry use cases.
</p>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><strong> 📸 🌈 APPN M3M — Multispectral & RGB UAV Phenotyping Platform</strong></summary>

<p>
The APPN M3M platform is a dedicated agricultural UAV system based on the <strong>DJI Mavic series</strong>, integrating a compact multispectral sensing suite with high‑resolution RGB imagery and centimetre‑accurate RTK positioning.
</p>

<p>
The M3M imaging payload includes:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Multispectral camera with 5&nbsp;MP sensors capturing <strong>blue, green, red, red‑edge, and near‑infrared</strong> bands</li>
<li>Integrated <strong>20&nbsp;MP RGB camera</strong> for visual interpretation and mapping</li>
<li>Onboard <strong>RTK GNSS</strong> for high positional accuracy</li>
</ul>

<p>
The system is lightweight, agile, and easily deployable, with long flight endurance relative to payload weight. These characteristics make the M3M particularly well suited to:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Rapid screening of large numbers of trials</li>
<li>Repeated monitoring through the growing season</li>
<li>Remote or logistically constrained field locations</li>
</ul>

</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><strong> 📸 🌈 🌿 APPN GOBI — RGB, VNIR & LiDAR UAV Phenotyping Platform</strong></summary>

<p>
The APPN GOBI platform centres on the <strong>GRYFN GOBI sensor package</strong>, providing a compact yet comprehensive instrumentation suite for <strong>high‑throughput digital phenotyping</strong> across crop trial sites and paddock‑scale experiments.
</p>

<p>
GOBI integrates co‑registered sensing technologies, including:
</p>

<ul>
<li><strong> VNIR hyperspectral imaging</strong> for quantitative spectral trait analysis</li>
<li><strong>Ouster LiDAR</strong> for canopy structure, height, and surface modelling</li>
<li><strong>Sony RGB camera</strong> for high‑resolution visual context</li>
</ul>

<p>
The GOBI payload can be deployed on either:
</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>DJI M350</strong> — offering rapid deployment and operational flexibility, or</li>
<li><strong>Inspired Flight IF1200</strong> — enabling heavier payload capacity and larger survey areas</li>
</ul>

<p>
All configurations are linked to <strong>RTK‑based GNSS‑INS positioning</strong>, supporting robust geometric alignment between LiDAR, hyperspectral, and RGB data streams. This enables reliable extraction of physical and structural traits at scale.
</p>

</details>

<hr>

<details>
  <summary><strong> 🌈🌈 🌿 APPN CALViS — Co‑aligned LiDAR, VNIR &amp; SWIR UAV Phenotyping Platform</strong></summary>

<p>
The APPN CALViS platform provides a highly integrated sensing solution for detailed physiological and structural analysis, combining long‑wave spectral coverage with 3‑D canopy characterisation.
</p>

<p>
CALViS integrates:
</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Headwall co‑aligned VNIR and SWIR hyperspectral sensors</strong></li>
<li><strong>LiDAR instrumentation</strong> for structural context and surface modelling</li>
<li>High‑accuracy GNSS‑INS positioning</li>
</ul>

<p>
Hyperspectral coverage spanning approximately <strong>350–2500&nbsp;nm</strong> enables analysis of phenotypic traits related to:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Disease detection and progression</li>
<li>Nutrient status and deficiency</li>
<li>Abiotic stress indicators (e.g. water or heat stress)</li>
</ul>

<p>
The co‑aligned sensor geometry ensures consistent spatial correspondence between VNIR, SWIR, and LiDAR observations, supporting advanced multi‑modal analysis and trait modelling.
</p>

</details>

<p>
This configuration delivers exceptionally high‑quality RGB imagery suitable for:
</p>

<ul>

<li>Canopy structure and morphology studies</li>
<li>Visual scoring, annotation, and validation</li>
</ul>
